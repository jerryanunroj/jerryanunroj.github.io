About Me

I am currently a research affiliate at Massachusetts Institute of Technology and Chulalongkorn University.

I received my A.M. in Statistics and A.B. in Applied Mathematics at Harvard University, where I was advised by Yiling Chen.

My research interests are at the interface between economics, learning, and algorithms.

Papers

Naive Bayesian Learning in Social Networks (EC 2018)

Jerry Anunrojwong, Nat Sothanaphan

The DeGroot model of naive social learning assumes that agents only communicate scalar opinions. In practice, agents communicate not only their opinions, but their confidence in such opinions. We propose a model that captures this aspect of communication by incorporating signal informativeness into the naive social learning scenario.
